The brainy hub

Here’s a stat for you: Kolkata is not just any city; it's home to the University of Calcutta, one of the oldest in Asia, producing thousands of graduates each year across fields like engineering, business, and the arts.

Rabindranath Tagore (1861-1941)

Bengali polymath. Referred by many as the Bengali Shakespeare. Most successful and influential writer in Bengali literature. First Nobel Prize winner from Asia. Composer of national anthems of India and Bangladesh.

With cultural giants like Rabindranath Tagore and Satyajit Ray as part of its alumni, Kolkata is more like the Harvard of arts and drama. It’s this intellectual environment that makes it a goldmine for outsourcing companies looking for a workforce that's not only skilled but insightful.

Jagadish Chandra Bose (1858-1937)

Major pioneer in researching radio and microwave optics. Made groundbreaking contributions to plant science. Laid the foundation of experimental science in Indian subcontinent.

Satyajit Ray (1921-1992)

Second Oscar winner from India. One of the most successful writers in modern age Bengali literature. Showed great talent in music and drawing.

Cost-effective without cutting corners

With a per capita income of roughly $1,800, significantly lower than the national average for major cities, Kolkata presents a compelling economic case for outsourcing.

This affordability, coupled with a literacy rate hovering around 87% (well above the national average), makes Kolkata an attractive hub for outsourcing companies looking to maximize value without skimping on talent.
